\section{Natural Transformations}

\begin{tikzcd}
  \mathcal{C} \arrow[r, bend left, "F"{name=F}]
  \arrow[r, bend right, "G"{name=G}] \arrow[d, from=F, to=G, "\eta"] & \mathcal{D}
\end{tikzcd}

$\eta$ is a natural transformation $\forall C \in obj(\mathcal{C})$ we have a morphism
\[
\begin{tikzcd}
  F(C) \arrow[r, "\eta_{C}"] & G(C)
\end{tikzcd}
\]
such that given $f: A \rtarw B$ in $\mathcal{C}$

\begin{tikzcd}
  F(A) \arrow[r, "\eta_{A}"] \arrow[d, "F(f)"] & G(A) \arrow[d, "G(f)"] \\
  F(B) \arrow[r, "\eta_{B}"] & G(B)
\end{tikzcd}

We also have the identity:
\begin{tikzcd}
  \mathcal{C} \arrow[r, bend left, "F"{name=F}]
  \arrow[r, bend right, "F"{name=G}] \arrow[d, from=F, to=G, "1_F"] & \mathcal{D}
\end{tikzcd}

Composition and communitivity work here too!

Exponential of Categories of Categories: $\mathcal{D}^\mathcal{C}$ -wow-
